Highlighting recommended shows

The Filter Sheet frame won; this study asks how For You matches should surface inside it. Recommendations come in two tiers with different confidence — loved (you hearted this artist on air) and similar (semantic-index affinity, “because you like X”) — and each treatment must express both. Toggle For You mode to see how each treatment behaves when the sheet's toggle narrows the list versus when matches sit among everything else.

Open questions

  • Tier weight. Loved and similar must not read as the same signal — a wrong “similar” hurts trust more than a missing one. Is the loved/similar contrast in each treatment strong enough at a glance?
  • Noise cap. As likes accumulate, similar matches could dominate the list. Cap similar highlights per window (e.g. top 3 by affinity score) and let the rest surface only in For You mode?
  • H2 duplication. The shelf duplicates shows that also appear in the date list below. Duplicate (scannable but repetitive) vs hoist-and-remove (no repeats but breaks date order)?
  • Reason phrasing. “Because you like Stereolab” asserts a private fact on screen. Fine on a personal device — but should it soften when the similarity is weak (“In the orbit of…”)?
  • Interplay with filters. When a genre/venue filter is active, do highlights persist inside the filtered subset (this study assumes yes) and does For You mode compose with other facets (assumed: yes, it's one more predicate)?
