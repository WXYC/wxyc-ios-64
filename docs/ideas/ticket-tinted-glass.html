The ticket, as tinted glass

Retiring the amber & cream-paper skin for a tinted Liquid-Glass ticket that takes its color from the active theme. The ticket metaphor lives in the shape — the perforation and the two side notches — not in paper texture, so we can drop the paper entirely and it still reads as a ticket.

Tints below are derived from each theme's real accent (HSB). Wallpapers are approximated (the real ones are Metal shaders) and kept hue-coherent so they judge the ticket, not the backdrop.

Why change it

Amber (#FF8940 ≈ hue 23°) is essentially the WXYC 1983 accent applied to every theme. Against the 7 of 9 themes whose accent is green / teal / cyan / blue, the fixed amber fights the wallpaper. Same ticket, one theme:

Three directions

All keep the glass body; they differ in what the stub does. Shown on Space Mountain (blue).

Cross-hue caveat for A. The two-tone look is gorgeous on cool accents, but a derived partner hue (here accent +28°) turns warm accents muddy — orange → chartreuse. So A only works if the stub's partner hue is authored per theme (serialized), whereas B and C derive cleanly from the accent alone.

Recommended — B, across all nine themes

Tinted body, clear-glass stub. The stub becomes the see-through “keepsake” — the piece you'd tear off — and it derives entirely from the accent, so every current and future theme just works.

C — mono-graduated, across all nine

The restrained alternative: one hue deepening across the seam; reads as a single solid object. Also pure-derivation.

How the tint is defined per theme

The accent already lives in every theme manifest. Three ways to turn it into ticket colors:

option 1

Derive from accent

Ticket tints are computed from accent at render time. Zero new JSON; every theme (and every future theme) is covered automatically.

recommended

Hybrid: derive + optional override

Derive by default, but allow an optional ticket block in a theme's manifest to hand-tune (needed if we ever want direction A's partner hue). Mirrors the existing per-theme override pattern.

option 3

Explicit per-theme palette

Author full ticket colors in all 9 manifests. Maximum control, most work, and new themes need hand-authoring or they fall back.

The CTA inherits the same tokens automatically — its “NEW” stamp roundel and stub labels re-tint from the accent, so the discovery card and the real ticket stay one visual object.
