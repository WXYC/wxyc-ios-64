The ticket feature, announcing itself

A discovery CTA that teaches the new Box Office ticket — so it's built as a ticket, stamped NEW . Same job as the Siri and theme tips: sit below the player, tell users what just landed, get dismissed. The message is the feature itself — when an artist you hear plays a show nearby, a ticket stub appears under their track. The feed does the surfacing; the CTA just teaches users what that stub means. No tap on the card.

How it behaves

Same mechanism as SiriTipView — an isVisible binding, an onDismiss , gated by DefaultsStorage . Only the chrome and the trigger differ.

  • shows Once, to teach the feature — new installs and existing users on the update that ships tickets.
  • retires The moment the user opens a real ticket in a track's details — the lesson's landed. (Mirrors the theme tip retiring once the picker's used.)
  • dismiss The X collapses it and sets ticketCTA.wasDismissed — gone for good.
  • tap None on the card. It points down at the feed, where marked rows carry their own stub.

What it reuses

The ticket chrome, promoted out of BoxOfficeTicketView into a shared component.

  • shape TicketShape — 16pt corners, two 11pt notches at the seam.
  • stub Cream keepsake + dashed perforation, unchanged.
  • right block The concert date becomes a rubber-stamp NEW — the card flags itself.
  • glass Liquid Glass on iOS 26+, .ultraThinMaterial fallback — same as the ticket.

Notes for the spec

  • Not a TipView . TipCard is a plain rounded rect; this needs the notched TicketShape . It's a sibling view mirroring SiriTipView 's isVisible + onDismiss surface but rendering ticket chrome.
  • Extract the chrome first. TicketShape , DashedLine , and the stub styling are private inside BoxOfficeTicketView . Promote them to a shared ticket component (Playlist package) so the CTA, the detail ticket, and the row badge share one geometry.
  • Retire on real use, not just dismiss. Track "has the user ever opened a real Box Office ticket." Once they have, the teaching card is redundant — suppress it even if never dismissed. This is the theme-tip pattern ( hasEverUsedPicker ), not the Siri one.
  • Don't stack on first launch. ThemeTipView.shouldShowTip is also true on a fresh install. Order these — the ticket CTA is the newest feature, so it likely leads; the theme tip waits its turn.
  • The stub is pure chrome. No date to format, so no presenter — the "NEW" stamp and the sub-labels are static. Simpler than the Box Office stub.
  • Don't tell users to hunt. The feed already badges touring artists with an inline stub ( OnTourRowBadge ), so copy like "tap any track to check" is misleading. The CTA's job is to explain the stub they'll passively encounter — not prescribe a manual search.
