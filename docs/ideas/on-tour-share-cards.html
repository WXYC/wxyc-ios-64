On Tour — what a shared show looks like

One listener taps Share on the Jessica Pratt show. This page renders what everyone on the other end sees — in iMessage with and without the app, with and without an App Clip, in WhatsApp and Slack, and in Safari. Every card below is a projection of a single URL; we author the projection once, in the share page's OG tags and (Phase 2) the App Clip's default-experience metadata. Architecture and phasing live in the companion doc docs/ideas/on-tour-sharing.md.

1

The link is the product

Everything ships as one canonical HTTPS universal link. Who taps it, and what they have installed, decides which of three experiences answers.

https://wxyc.org/shows/4821
host — wxyc.orgThe zone is already on Cloudflare. Worker routes on /shows/* and the exact AASA path serve the share pages and association file; GitHub Pages keeps everything else (including ACME renewals). Links inherit the station's domain trust and outlive any app-store URL.
path — /shows/Human word, not the API's "concerts" — this string is visible in every SMS fallback and copy-paste. Reserved exclusively for share targets.
id — 4821Backend concerts.id. An optional readable suffix (/shows/4821-jessica-pratt) is tolerated and ignored by the parser.
has the app

Universal link → the app

iOS routes the tap straight into WXYC. The existing NSUserActivityTypeBrowsingWeb handler parses it, switches to the On Tour tab, and presents this show's poster detail.

no app · phase 2

App Clip card → the clip

The AASA appclips entry + an App Store Connect experience turn the same link into a native App Clip card in Messages and Safari. Poster detail + tickets + a foreground listen, no install.

no app · always

Share page → Safari

The Worker renders the OG-tagged show page: poster hero, ticket link, a live-stream web player, and the Smart App Banner pointing at the app (or the clip). This is the floor every other surface degrades to.

contacts requirementMessages shows the App Clip card only when the sender is in the recipient's contacts. Otherwise: the plain rich link (state A/C). The share page must carry the experience either way.

default card onlyMessages always renders the default experience's metadata, even when an advanced experience matches the URL. Card art is station-generic + og-image; per-show art lives in the rich-link path.

Reading the matrix: Phase 1 (universal link + share page + OG tags) fully covers seven of eight rows. The App Clip upgrades exactly one cell — iMessage, no app, sender-in-contacts — plus the Safari banner variant. That cell is likely the most common share (friends texting friends), which is the argument for Phase 2; the instrumentation on Phase 1's share page tells us how common, which is the argument for sequencing it second.
Mockups are hand-drawn approximations of iOS 26 chrome; fidelity is directional, not pixel-exact. Mechanics verified 2026-07-18 against Apple docs: Messages invocations & Smart App Banner, AASA appclips association, App Clip capability limits (no Background Modes), size limits (100 MB digital / 15 MB physical).
