How each maps to iOS 26

All four share the B arrival (.matchedTransitionSource on the row → .navigationTransition(.zoom) into the destination) and the same content blocks. Only the destination's scroll structure differs — and each is stock SwiftUI.

B1 · Immersive

Art pinned, panel over

Art in a .visualEffect/GeometryReader-driven parallax layer; the details ride a rounded panel with .background(.ultraThinMaterial) that scrolls up over it. Closest to Apple Music's now-playing.

Cost: CTA lives in a bottom dock far below the hero.

B2 · Tucked Ticket

Overlap with a negative inset

Poster hero, then the ticket card pulled up with .offset(y:)/.padding(.top,-34) so it straddles the seam. Reuses the shipped BoxOfficeTicketView geometry as the card.

CTA sits on the card at thumb height — no separate dock needed.

B3 · Editorial

Type is the design

Condensed uppercase name, ruled credits, a rotated "SPUN ON WXYC" stamp. Mostly text + Divider() — the poster art is a duotone backdrop. Highest personality, highest craft risk (legibility, Dynamic Type, a11y).

The credits/stamp voice is worth borrowing into any layout.

B4 · Collapsing

Hero → sticky header

A pinnedScrollableViews / scroll-offset header that fades to solid once the hero scrolls past. The most scalable for long content and the gentlest when artwork is weak.

Least distinctive — reads like a well-made standard product page.

Open questions

  • Where does the CTA live? B2/B3/B4 keep Get Tickets thumb-reachable (tucked card, sticky ink bar, sticky header pill); B1 buries it in a bottom dock. If a pinned CTA is a hard requirement, B1 needs a dock that's always visible, not just on scroll.
  • How load-bearing is the artwork? B1 and B3 lean hardest on the image — they look thin over a generated gradient. B2 and B4 degrade gracefully when image_url is null (the ticket / the modular stack carry the page). That may decide it before taste does.
  • Do we keep the Box Office ticket at all? B2 says yes (it's the tucked card); B1/B3/B4 dissolve it into blocks + a plain CTA. That's a real product question — the ticket is a keepsake with its own equity across the app (playcut surfaces, the CTA study #481).
  • Editorial per-show, or one system? B3 is gorgeous for one hand-tuned show but the type scale has to survive a 4-word artist name, a festival title, and Dynamic Type XXL. Is the ruled-credits block enough personality without the giant name?
  • Status treatments. Sold-out / cancelled need to read on a poster, not just a ticket. Cancelled here desaturates; sold-out keeps the pill. Confirm that survives over real photography.
