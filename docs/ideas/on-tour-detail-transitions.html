What happens when you tap a show?

Today the On Tour tab presents a tapped show as a plain large sheet holding one Box Office ticket — which reads barren, and the slide-up says nothing about the row you came from. Below are four ways to make the tap feel intentional and fill the screen. Flip between Now (the current barren sheet) and each proposal — the device is live: tap any row, watch the transition, use the back / Done control to return.

Transition

Tap a show in the phone. ← → switch transition · Esc closes the detail.

How each maps to iOS 26

Every transition here is buildable with stock SwiftUI on our deployment target — none needs UIKit or a third-party lib. The presentation content (provenance, the bill, where, more-shows) is shared and variant-independent; it is the part that actually cures "barren," and it can land first, under the current sheet, before we pick a transition.

A · Hero Ticket

Zoom navigation

Row carries .matchedTransitionSource(id:in:); the destination pushes with .navigationTransition(.zoom(sourceID:in:)). The row visibly grows into the ticket, so the tap has a physical throughline.

Keeps BoxOfficeTicketView exactly as shipped — this is a wrapper + a scroll of content beneath, not a rewrite.

B · Poster

Zoom into full-bleed

Same .zoom source, but the destination is an image_url hero with a .scrollTransition parallax and a sticky glass action bar. Gated on real event artwork — image_url is on the schema but usually null; needs a scraper/enrichment pass first.

Fallback when art is missing: a generated gradient keyed on the venue slug (we already reserve slug as a tint seed).

C · Expand

No navigation

A @State selectedID in the LazyVStack; the row reveals an inline drawer under withAnimation(.spring). Zero new screens, zero sheet. Best if the tab's job is quick triage.

Cost: the drawer's room is small, so deep content (map, more-shows) still wants a "See full details" push.

D · Peek Sheet

Evolved sheet

The smallest diff from today: .presentationDetents([.height(300), .large]) + .presentationBackgroundInteraction so the list stays live behind the peek. Essentials at the peek, full story on drag-up.

Reuses the exact .sheet(item:) plumbing already in TouringTabView.

Open questions

  • Is the ticket the hero, or the artist? A treats the Box Office ticket as the destination (keepsake-first); B treats the artist/poster as the destination (discovery-first) and demotes the ticket to an action bar. That is the real fork — the transition follows from it.
  • Do we have artwork? B is the most striking but only if image_url is populated. Right now most scraped rows have none. Worth a spike on triangle-shows / Ticketmaster image coverage before committing to a poster-first detail.
  • Provenance is the WXYC-only move. "You heard them on WXYC · last spun Tue 9:14 PM" is what makes this detail ours and not a generic ticketing page — but it needs an airplay-match signal (headlining_artist_id against play history). Cheap version: "in your library / recently played"; rich version waits on the R3 likes/semantic work already planned.
  • One-handed reachability. A and B put the primary CTA far down a scroll; D and C keep it thumb-height. Do we need the CTA pinned regardless of variant (a sticky Get Tickets bar)?
  • Back-swipe. The zoom transition (A/B) reverses on interactive pop for free; the sheet (Now/D) and inline (C) each have their own dismiss gesture. Consistency across the tab matters if some detail paths push and others present.
