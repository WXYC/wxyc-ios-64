Box Office · surface studies

Barcode gone. The question was the surface — a black slab fights the app's glass-over-wallpaper world. Here the ticket body is warm glass the wallpaper glows through, and the stub is the one opaque, printed keepsake. The perforation notches are cut clean through to the gradient. Three ways to render that surface, judged against the live wallpaper.

Surface

Anatomy

Two materials + a window. That's the whole idea.

Body · warm smoked glass

The app's own backdrop-filter blur, tinted amber instead of cool. The wallpaper reads through it, so the card belongs to the same world as Release and streaming — just warmer.

Stub · opaque keepsake

The one solid, printed surface. Light where the body is dark, it pulls the eye to the date and earns the word "stub." The body/stub contrast is what makes it read as a ticket.

Perforation · window to the wallpaper

A dashed tear line with two notches cut through to the gradient (CSS mask, not paint). Those holes stitch the object back into the background it floats on.

Look & feel Recommended

Same anatomy, three surfaces — flip the switch and watch each one meet the wallpaper.

Amber Glass. Warm translucent body over a cream stub. Sits most naturally in the app — it's visibly the same glass as every other card, only warm. The wallpaper glows through the body and punches through the notches. Safest, most native. My pick.

Warm Paper. An opaque cardstock body with a dark stub — a real paper ticket dropped onto the feed. Highest contrast, most "object," most tactile. The boldest read; the risk is it feels like a sticker pasted over the glass rather than part of it.

Wallpaper Glass. The body is a concentrated, saturated slice of the app's own indigo→magenta→red gradient. It composes by being made of the wallpaper — richest and most colorful, though the amber signal is quieter since the surface itself is doing the color.

Unchanged for all three

  • One amber signal. The warm surface + amber CTA say "on tour" without ever touching the green on-air dot.
  • Match on a name, guarded. Triangle Shows has no artist ID — highlight only on a normalized exact match against GET /api/events?search={artist}&start={today}; treat fuzzy hits as no-match for v1.
  • State-aware. status drives the pill (on_sale / sold_out / cancelled / free); only ever show date >= today, soonest first.
  • Notch fidelity. The mask cut is real transparency, so it reveals whatever the wallpaper is doing at that scroll position — no color to keep in sync.
