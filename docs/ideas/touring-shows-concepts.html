Now Spinning → Playing Soon

When a track on the flowsheet is by a band with an upcoming show at a Triangle venue — Cat's Cradle, Local 506, Haw River Ballroom — we surface it right where you're already listening. Three directions for how that highlight looks in the playlist and the track detail. Flip between them.

Direction

Three directions, one signal

All three reuse the app's accent hue (23° amber) so the "on tour" signal reads as native — and stays clearly distinct from the green on-air dot. They differ in footprint: how much they ask of a dense, fast-scrolling feed.

A · Tactile

Ticket Stub

A torn-off stub with punched notches and a dashed perforation. The show reads as a physical object clipped to the track — the most gig-poster, the most memorable.

Best when the feature is a headline moment we want people to notice and screenshot.

B · Typographic

Marquee

Leans entirely on the app's existing small-caps monospaced label system: an eyebrow, a thin amber rule, a venue set in wide caps, a text link. Sits quietest inside the current glass UI.

Best when we want it to feel like it was always part of the app.

C · Minimal

Glow Chip

A single amber pill in the feed and one compact card in detail. Lowest visual footprint, least clutter risk in a busy flowsheet, fastest to ship and tune.

Best when matches are frequent and we can't afford to crowd the list.

Notes for the integration

  • Match key is a name, not an ID. Triangle Shows stores no MusicBrainz/Discogs ID — lookups are ILIKE on artist/name against a normalized string. To avoid a false "Nilüfer Yanya is playing" on a wrong match, highlight only on a normalized exact match (lowercased, punctuation-stripped) and treat fuzzy hits as no-match for v1.
  • The feed shape barely changes. Enrich each flowsheet Playcut with an optional upcomingShow object (venue, city, date, doors/show, price range, status, ticket URL) resolved server-side against GET /api/events?search={artist}&start={today}. The iOS row/detail code just checks for its presence.
  • It's a local signal. Triangle Shows only covers ~21 Raleigh–Durham–Chapel Hill venues, which is the whole charm — "playing near you" is literally true for WXYC's audience. Don't generalize copy to imply national tour dates.
  • Freshness & state. Data refreshes every 6h and status is mutable (on_sale / sold_out / cancelled / free) — reflect sold-out/cancelled in the pill, and only ever show date >= today.
  • Multiple upcoming shows. If an artist has more than one date, surface the soonest in the row and list the rest in detail.
